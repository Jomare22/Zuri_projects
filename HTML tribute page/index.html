Edward Christopher Sheeran

Many people may know the English Grammy-winning singer-songwriter, musician, actor and former rapper, Ed Sheeran, for his hit songs like 'Thinking Out Loud,' 'Photograph,' 'Shape of You' and 'Perfect'. But very few know how he came to be which is also the reason why I admire him.

His story enspires to never give up on your dreams. He started playing guitar and singing at a local church by the age of four. He was bullied at school for being bad at sports, having orange hair, for stuttering and wearing big, thick-framed glasses. He had a surgery which went wrong and left him with a lazy eye and serious speech problems. At the age of ten, he cured his stuttering by rapping along to Eminem's 'Marshall Matters' LP. He had his first live gig at the age of eleven. He recorded CD's as a teenager which he sold from his backpack. He moved to Londen at the age of fourteen and was homeless for two and a half years - sleeping outside Buckingham Palace - and not being able to afford food. Despite his rough circumstances and having to sleep on circle line trains between his performances, he didn't give up. Instead he tried even harder and captured the attention of Example who invited him on his tour. He moved to Los Angeles without a place to stay or any connections. It took several acts of sending his music to everyone to finally be brought on stage by a local radio host. It was there where he was spotted by Jamie Foxx's manager who introduced the two to each other. Jamie Foxx was so impressed by Ed and invited him to stay in his home and use his recording studio where he was able to produce his '+' album and things started going in his direction.

Here's a timeline highlighting some of his pre-fame events:

  • 1991 - Ed Sheeran was born on 17 February in Halifax, West Yorkshire, England.
  • 2000 - Ed learned to play the guitar after he was given a guitar by his uncle.
  • 2005 - Ed Sheeran began recoring at the age of 13. He released his first EP, The Orange Room.
  • 2006 - Ed Sheeran released his first album which was self titled.
  • 2007 - Ed Sheeran released the album 'Want some'.
  • 2008 - Ed Sheeran moved to London to pursue his music career and started playing at small venues.
  • 2009 - Ed Sheeran began studying music at the Academy of Contemporary Music in Guildford, Surrey. He played 312 gigs in an attempt to beat James Morrison's record of playing 200 gigs in one year.
  • 2010 - Ed Sheeran posted a recording of his song 'You need me' on YouTube. It got noticed and lead to rapper Example asking Ed on tour with him.
  • 2011 - Ed Sheeran released his final independent EP, No. 5 Collaborations Project, which features Wiley, JME, Devlin, Sway and Ghetts. His first single, The A Team, entered the UK Singles Chart at number three. His second single, You Need Me, I Don't Need You, was released on 28 August and peaked at number four on the UK Singles Chart. His debut album, +, was released on the 12 September and sold 102000 copies within the first week.
  • 2012 - Ed Sheeran co-wrote Taylor Swift's song 'Everything Has Changed' which featured on her album 'Red' and was released as a single in 2013. He also wrote two songs for One Direction's album 'Take Me Home' including their single 'Little Things' which was released in December 2012 and reached number 1 in the UK and number 18 in the US.
